Belarus

Belarus, officially the Republic of Belarus, is a state in Eastern Europe. The population as of January 1, 2023 is 9,200,617 people, the territory is 207.6 thousand km². It ranks 97th in terms of population and 84th in terms of territory in the world. The capital and largest city-state is Minsk. The official languages are Belarusian and Russian. The division occurs into six regions; The city of Minsk is a branch of an administrative-territorial unit and has the special status of a city of republican subordination.

Story

The date of the emergence of Belarusian statehood is considered to be January 1, 1919, when the Manifesto of the Provisional Workers' and Peasants' Government of Belarus proclaimed the Belarusian Socialist Soviet Republic within the RSFSR. The proclamation of the Executive Committee of the Belarusian People's Republic of the Rada of the All-Belarusian Congress, which preceded this on March 9, 1918, did not lead to the creation of full-fledged functioning authorities due to the German territory of Belarus

In 1922, the BSSR became part of the Soviet Union. In March 1924, the USSR Central Executive Committee decided to transfer 15 districts and individual volosts of the Vitebsk, Gomel and Smolensk provinces to the BSSR.

In the 1920s and 1930s, industrialization processes were actively underway in Soviet Belarus. By the beginning of industrialization, 3.4% of the population lived in the BSSR and only 1.6% of the industrial output of the USSR was produced. The light, food, woodworking and chemical industries developed everywhere, starting with the second Five-Year Plan - mechanical engineering and the production of building materials. Over the 3 five-year plans, industrial production in the BSSR increased 23 times.

Geographical position

The total area of Belarus is 207.6 thousand km², of which 202.9 thousand km² are on land. It is almost twice the size of Bulgaria, three times the size of Ireland. The countries closest in area are Great Britain and Romania. Belarus occupies a territory whose shape resembles a pentagon. From north to south it extends for 560 km, from west to east - for 650 km. The depth of the territory of Belarus is 220 km. The total length of the state border is 3617 km. The country borders on five countries: Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Lithuania and Latvia. The longest section of the border with Russia is 1283 km. The border with Ukraine is slightly shorter - 1084 km. In the west, for 398 km, Belarus borders with Poland, in the north-west - with Lithuania (679 km) and Latvia (173 km).

Belarus is located within the temperate geographical zone between 51 and 56° N. w. The extreme northern point is located in the Verkhnedvinsky district north of Lake Osveyskoye, and the southern one is in the Braginsky district near the urban village of Komarin. The westernmost point is located near the city of Vysokoye, Kamenets region, and the eastern point is near Khotimsk. The geographical center of the country is located near the village of Antonovo, Pukhovichi district and has coordinates 53°32′ N. w. 28°03′ E. d.HGYAO. According to Belarusian scientists, the geographical center of Europe is located on the territory of the country and is located near Polotsk, where a corresponding memorial sign has been installed

Economy

Belarus is a moderately developed industrial-agrarian state. The economy in Belarus is built on the principles of a socially oriented market model. The structure of the Belarusian economy is characterized by the predominance of state ownership in the production, energy, transport, mining, construction, agricultural and banking sectors, and a small share of the private sector. There is no centralized distribution and planning, except for macroeconomic indicators. The state regulates prices for socially significant groups of goods.

The country has developed energy, mechanical engineering, agriculture, chemical, forestry and mining industries, construction and production of building materials. The problem of Belarus is the lack of open sources of hydrocarbons (for example, oil production in 2012 amounted to 1.66 million tons), as a result of which a significant part of the oil and gas has to be imported. The republic also has an impressive and constantly growing external public debt (17.8 billion US dollars as of August 1, 2020) and fairly high inflation by European standards. Belarus spends significant amounts of money on payments on public debt - 6.8 billion rubles or 34% of the republican budget in 2019, however, the main amount of it is refinanced; the cost of servicing public debt as a result in 2019 amounted to 2.34 billion rubles.
